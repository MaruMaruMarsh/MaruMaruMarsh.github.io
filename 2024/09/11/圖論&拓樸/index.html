Paths

Non-empty graph G=(V,E)G=(V,E)G=(V,E)

Cycles

A circuit with no repeated edges except the first and last

  • Girth: The minimum length of a cycle
  • Circumference: The maximum length of a cycle
  • Chord: An edge joining two vertices of a cycle but not part of the cycle
  • Distance: The length of the shortest path between two vertices
  • Diameter: The maximum distance between two vertices

1.5 Trees and forests

forest / tree / leaves

A acyclic graph is a forest. A connected forest is a tree. A vertex of degree 1 is a leaf

root / tree-order

  • root is the least element in its tree-order
  • Define a partial ordering on the vertices of a tree TTT, where x≤yx\leq yx≤y for x∈rTyx\in rTyx∈rTy.
    As an expression of height: ⌊x⌋:={y∣y≥x}\lfloor x \rfloor := \{y|y\geq x\}⌊x⌋:={y∣y≥x}(down-closure of y) and ⌈y⌉:={x∣y≥x}\lceil y \rceil := \{x|y\geq x\}⌈y⌉:={x∣y≥x}(up-closure of x)

    背法：⌈y⌉\lceil y \rceil⌈y⌉的話，yyy是最高的，所以xxx是包含yyy的所有點；⌊x⌋\lfloor x \rfloor⌊x⌋的話，xxx是最低的，所以是包含yyy的所有點

height / level / normal tree

  • chain: the down-closure of every vertex is a chain
  • height: the vertices at distance kkk from the root have height kkk and form the kkk-th level of TTT
  • normal tree: a rooted tree TTT contained in a graph GGG is called normal if the ends of every TTT-path are comparable in the tree-order of TTT
